For-Ever-Green
----------------------------------------------
projection installation / visual art / interactive
keywords:
Vegetation / Manipulation / Highrise / Living Condition

How can we use projection to reveal that developers distort nature into artificial exoticism to increase the buildings’ value?

This is a social design project that critiques how developers manipulate plants as a strategy to create an artificial exotic illusion in terms of increasing the building’s value. Architects attempt to use artificial climate to create an exotic space in highrise. However, space is filled with the desire to manipulate plants’ spices, color, and height. As a fantasy landscape, high-rise garden is like a college that brings evergreen plants to the modern city, but they lost their original character. To manipulate plants in an isolated artificial condition, the high-rise gardens can maintenance the most beautiful appearance of the vegetation which human would appreciate. For-Ever-Green is a installation that project distorted moving images of highrise on cylindrical surface. The installation creates an isolated artificial exotic space for the audience to experience the power of manipulation of plants.
